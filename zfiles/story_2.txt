Once upon a time, in a beautiful green forest, there lived a little sparrow named Chikki. Chikki was very small, but she had a big heart and a curious mind.

Every day, Chikki would fly around the forest, watching the other animals. She saw the strong elephant, the fast deer, and the clever monkey. Chikki often felt sad and thought, “I am so small. I cannot do big things like them.”

One hot afternoon, the forest animals were resting near a big tree. Suddenly, a small fire started in the dry grass. The wind began blowing, and the fire slowly started spreading.

The animals became scared.

“Run! Run!” shouted the deer.
“We must leave the forest!” cried the monkey.

But Chikki did not want her home to burn.

She flew quickly to the nearby river, dipped her tiny beak in the water, and dropped a small drop of water on the fire.

The other animals looked at her and laughed.

“Chikki, your drops are too small,” said the monkey. “You cannot stop the fire!”

But Chikki kept flying again and again—
river to fire, river to fire—
dropping tiny drops of water.

Seeing her courage, the elephant felt ashamed.

“If a tiny sparrow is trying so hard, we should help too,” he said.

The elephant carried big trunks of water, the deer stamped on the flames, and the monkeys brought wet leaves.

Together, they stopped the fire before it spread across the forest.

All the animals cheered for Chikki.

The wise old owl said,
“Never think you are too small to make a difference. Even the smallest effort can inspire others.”

Chikki smiled happily, because she had saved her home and learned something important.
